Making Learning Interactive with Coursera's Dialogue

Dialogue are course activities that enable authors to create AI-driven discussions, simulating interactions with a live instructor.

This approach leads students through structured, text-based conversations designed to achieve specific learning objectives. Dialogue are an additional tool in our teaching toolkit strategically positioned to activate learners, support critical thinking, and improve knowledge application while keeping instructors central.

L.E.A.R.N. Best Practices

L - Leverage Prior Knowledge

Assess prior knowledge and help learners set clear learning goals. Build on existing understanding foundations.

E - Engage Through Scenarios

Connect learning to practical scenarios. Guide discovery through strategic questioning. Simulate real-world applications.

A - Activate Participation

Create interactive, participatory experiences. Encourages learners to think aloud and reason through problems systematically.

R - Reinforce Concepts

Reinforce and review key concepts to strengthen retention. Provides immediate responses that help learners self-correct their thinking.

N - Navigate Understanding

Clarify difficult concepts and prepare students for evaluation. Open-ended questioning reveals what students don't yet understand.

Why Dialogue Work

Targeted Learning

Scenario-based prompts transform abstract ideas into concrete understanding.

Active Engagement

Create interactive, participatory experiences that keep learners involved.

Practical Application

Simulate real-world applications and practical implementation.

Continuous Improvement

Provide detailed feedback for improvement and self-correction.

Where to Place Dialogue

Start of Modules

Place after lessons to revisit concepts and strengthen retention. Start modules by uncovering what learners know and their goals.

Before Projects

Insert before projects to connect ideas to real applications and practical scenarios.

Assessment Points

Use before exams or after to guide reflection and improvement. Clarify difficult concepts.
